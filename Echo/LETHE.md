# Echo — verdict: LETHE (honest limitation / constrained validation)

CP3 routed to Lethe under the locked gate. This is a **valid verdict**, not a failure: Echo's
method works exactly as designed and returns a clear, tightly-bounded negative result about
the *deployed interval*. All numbers below are regenerated by `scripts/run_validation.py`
(seed 20260613) into `results/RESULTS_VALIDATION.{json,md}`; they are **PROVISIONAL** on
Fashion/Gauge/Minos (see `ASSUMPTIONS.md`).

## The finding (one sentence)
A conformal IVIM interval scale-matched to the *thermal* ROI-mean measurement regime is
**~4× too narrow** to contain the real same-day test–retest variation of the diffusion
coefficient D: at the headline calibration, test–retest coverage of D is **0.263
[BCa 95% 0.158, 0.355]** against the analytic target of **0.755** for a correctly-scaled
interval (scale ratio R = σ_implied/σ_repeat = **0.247**).

## Why this is Lethe and not PASS
The locked CP0 gate required coverage_D's 95% CI to exclude 0.65 and 1.00 with the point in
[0.72, 0.96]. Observed coverage_D = 0.263 (CI [0.158, 0.355]) is **under-scaled** — and the
pre-registered sensitivity curve shows it stays under-scaled across Gauge's *entire*
single-voxel SNR grid (0.434 at SNR 10 → 0.053 at SNR 100); the SNR that would reach 0.755
lies outside any plausible grid. The verdict is therefore **robust to the calibration
assumption**, not an artifact of the headline choice. No tuning was applied.

## Exactly what Echo validated, and what it could not
- **Validated (precision):** Echo *correctly measures* whether a deployed interval is sized
  to measurement irreproducibility. The synthetic self-test (`run_harness.py`, SOLID) proves
  the statistic recovers the analytic coverage, is invariant to bias, and is distinct from
  Gauge's rank check. On real data it delivers a clean coverage estimate with BCa CIs.
- **Could not (accuracy):** Echo is provably blind to accuracy/bias (Δ cancels common bias).
  The real D estimates are themselves biased low by the high-b Rician floor — Echo neither
  sees nor corrects this, by construction. Echo makes **no ground-truth coverage claim**.
- **The negative result itself:** real same-day test–retest variation of ROI-mean D
  (σ_repeat ≈ 2.0×10⁻⁴ mm²/s; within-subject CV ≈ 54%) vastly exceeds the thermal-noise scale
  a conformal interval is calibrated to (σ_implied ≈ 0.5×10⁻⁴). I.e. **real repeatability is
  dominated by non-thermal sources** (repositioning, ROI re-segmentation, noise-floor
  interaction) that synthetic conformal calibration cannot see.

## The explicit delta vs Gauge §4.2.2
Gauge showed the interval *width* RANK-tracks scan–rescan scatter (Spearman D r=+0.60
[0.42,0.72]; D* null). Echo shows the interval *scale* does **not** match that scatter
(coverage 0.263, R≈0.25). These are consistent and complementary: **rank-tracking ≠
scale-calibration.** A width that correctly *orders* repeatability can still be the *wrong
size* for it. This is exactly the axis Gauge declined to test, and the reason Echo is not a
duplicate.

The D* row corroborates from the other side: D* is so unidentified that its interval is ~20×
*wider* than D* repeatability (coverage 0.797, R=19.5) — over-scaled by the identifiability
wall. The D (under-scaled) / D* (over-scaled-by-unidentifiability) split is itself the
signature that the conformal scale is not governed by repeatability.
