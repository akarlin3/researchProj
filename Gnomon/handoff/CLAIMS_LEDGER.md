## KEEP — reproduced, carry to the retool as-is (with CIs)

### K1 · NLLS D\* boundary-railing on real in-vivo data  ·  **primary-claim candidate**
- **Fashion claim:** "54.7% of 1618 high-SNR ROI voxels have a boundary-railed NLLS
  D\* estimate" (REVIEWER_RESPONSE_R2.md).
- **Evidence (run):** independent box-constrained NLLS on the open OSIPI abdomen
  homogeneous ROI (Zenodo 14605039, sha256-verified): **54.2% [52.0, 56.4]** railed
  (rail_tol 1e‑3); **56.2% [54.0, 58.4]** (1e‑2); **58.7%** for the b0‑SNR>25 subset.
  Claimed 54.7% sits inside the CI.
- **Reworded claim:** *"On open in-vivo abdominal IVIM data, an independent box-
  constrained NLLS rails the D\* estimate at a fit bound in 54.2% [52.0, 56.4] of
  homogeneous-ROI voxels (vs a prior report of 54.7%), a per-voxel signature of weak
  pseudo-diffusion identifiability."*

### K2 · The interval *shape* (quantiles), not a wider SD, restores marginal D\* coverage  ·  **primary-claim candidate**
- **Fashion claim:** D\* coverage at nominal 0.95 is 0.94 with the MCMC 2.5/97.5
  quantile interval (vs 0.30/0.67 for symmetric SD) — README.md.
- **Evidence (run):** synthetic 9-cell set; MCMC quantile-interval **marginal** D\*
  coverage **0.90 [0.89, 0.91]**; D **0.95 [0.94, 0.96]**, f **0.96 [0.96, 0.97]**.
- **Reworded claim:** *"Reading the posterior 2.5/97.5 quantile interval rather than a
  symmetric SD interval restores near-nominal **marginal** D\* coverage (0.90 [0.89,
  0.91]); D and f are already near-nominal. The right interval **shape**, not a larger
  SD, is the fix."* (Residual high-D\* gap: see R2.)

### K3 · Amortized (MAF/NPE) posterior beats the railed NLLS on calibration *and* sharpness  ·  **primary-claim candidate**
- **Fashion claim:** the flow posterior is sharper and better-calibrated than the
  railed-NLLS baseline (ECE/sharpness behavior; README defines, gives no numbers).
- **Evidence (run):** synthetic held-out set, D\*: flow coverage **0.979** vs NLLS
  **0.763** (gap 0.216 [0.197, 0.235]); ECE **0.069** vs **0.121** (gap 0.052 [0.036,
  0.075]); sharpness **0.112** vs **0.181** (gap 0.068 [0.060, 0.077]). All three gaps'
  CIs exclude 0.
- **Reworded claim:** *"A clean-room amortized (neural posterior) estimator is
  simultaneously better-calibrated (D\* coverage 0.98 vs 0.76), lower-ECE (0.069 vs
  0.121), and sharper (0.11 vs 0.18) than the boundary-railed NLLS baseline — the
  railed Gaussian's wide-but-miscentred intervals lose on every axis."*

## REFRAME — real phenomenon, but the marginal headline number is not regime/convention-robust

### R1 · Symmetric Gaussian intervals under-cover D\*  ·  marginal → **conditional**
- **Fashion claim (headline):** D\* coverage 0.30 (Laplace SD) / 0.67 (MCMC SD) at
  nominal 0.95 — "severely overconfident."
- **Why reframed:** the *marginal* 0.30/0.67 do not reproduce (clean rebuild: Laplace
  **0.80 [0.78, 0.82]**, MCMC-SD **0.90 [0.89, 0.92]**). They reappear only under an
  undocumented hard (high-D\*) cohort and an undocumented overconfident SD convention
  (R3). The honest, reproducible statement is **per-true-D\*-tercile** (honest CRLB):

  | Estimator (honest CRLB) | low D\* | mid D\* | **high D\*** | pooled |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | Laplace SD | 0.91 [0.89,0.94] | 0.86 [0.83,0.89] | **0.63 [0.60,0.67]** | 0.80 [0.78,0.82] |
  | MCMC SD | 0.95 [0.93,0.97] | 0.95 [0.94,0.97] | **0.81 [0.78,0.84]** | 0.90 [0.89,0.92] |

- **Reworded claim:** *"Symmetric Gaussian intervals under-cover D\* in the high-D\*
  regime: under an honest CRLB, central-95% D\* coverage falls to 0.63 [0.60, 0.67]
  (Laplace SD) and 0.81 [0.78, 0.84] (MCMC SD) in the high-D\* tercile, versus ~0.9 in
  the low-D\* tercile — an identifiability-driven, conditional failure (Methods: SD
  convention)."*

### R2 · The quantile fix is *marginal*, not conditional — a residual high-D\* wall remains
- **Fashion claim (implicit):** the quantile interval "fixes" D\* coverage.
- **Why reframed:** the fix is at the **marginal** level (K2: 0.90). Conditionally, the
  MCMC quantile interval still under-covers the high-D\* tercile: **0.81 [0.78, 0.84]**
  (vs 0.93 low, 0.97 mid). Consistent with Gauge's irreducible high-D\* wall.
- **Reworded claim:** *"The quantile interval restores marginal coverage but does not
  close the high-D\* gap (tercile coverage 0.81 [0.78, 0.84]); the residual is the
  identifiability wall, not an interval-shape artifact."*

### R3 · "Below-CRLB-floor overconfidence" of the reported SD  ·  tie to the SD convention; re-run honest
- **Fashion claim:** reported NPE SDs sit below the CRLB floor for 69.5% (clinical-
  sparse) / 41.4% (dense) of D\* grid points (REVIEWER_RESPONSE.md). **Gnomon did not
  re-run this number.**
- **Why reframed (not asserted):** this is the same axis as the SD convention. Gnomon
  *did* run the convention's effect on coverage: flooring a railed/unidentified D\*'s
  SD (the overconfident convention) drops Laplace D\* coverage to pooled **0.68 [0.65,
  0.70]** / high-tercile **0.41 [0.37, 0.45]** — i.e. the floored convention manufactures
  the original severity. The honest CRLB widens where information is absent.
- **Reworded claim (pending re-run):** *"Whether reported SDs fall 'below the CRLB
  floor' is an artifact of the railed-voxel SD convention; report it under the honest
  CRLB (Methods §5) or state the floor convention explicitly."* The retool should
  re-run the 69.5%/41.4% figures under the honest convention before quoting them.
