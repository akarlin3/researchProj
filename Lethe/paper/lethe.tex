\begin{abstract}
\noindent
A conformal prediction interval can be validated for marginal coverage only where a
ground-truth label exists. In quantitative MRI it usually does not. The one label-free
signal that is genuinely available is \emph{repeatability}: same-session scan--rescan
acquisitions. We ask a precise, and deliberately narrow, question of a deployed conformal
intravoxel-incoherent-motion (IVIM) interval: is it the right \emph{size} to capture a
measurement's own irreproducibility? We answer it with \emph{test--retest interval
coverage} --- whether one scan's parameter estimate falls inside the other scan's deployed
interval --- reported with bias-corrected-and-accelerated (BCa) bootstrap confidence
intervals. The statistic is \emph{precision, not accuracy}: the test--retest difference
cancels any bias common to both scans, so it certifies that an interval is scaled to
measurement noise and is provably blind to systematic error. It is also distinct from, and
orthogonal to, the published width-\emph{rank}-tracks-repeatability check\PROV{} (Spearman
$r=\numGaugeR$): rescaling every width by a constant leaves the rank correlation invariant
but moves coverage arbitrarily. On a synthetic harness the statistic recovers its analytic
behaviour exactly. On $\numN$ real same-day test--retest breast-DWI tumors (ACRIN-6698),
the conformally deployed interval for the well-identified diffusion coefficient $D$ is
about four times too \emph{narrow} to contain real scan--rescan variation: coverage
$\numCovD$ (BCa $95\%$ $[\numCovDlo,\numCovDhi]$) against an analytic target of
$\numTarget$, scale ratio $R=\numRD$, under-scaled across the entire plausible
signal-to-noise range. We conclude --- as a constrained validation --- that conventionally
calibrated conformal IVIM intervals \emph{order} repeatability correctly but are not
\emph{sized} to it, because region-level repeatability is dominated by non-thermal sources
that synthetic calibration does not model.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
Distribution-free conformal prediction gives finite-sample marginal coverage \emph{when a
held-out label is available}. For quantitative MRI parameter maps --- here the IVIM triplet
$(D,\Dstar,f)$ --- no per-voxel ground truth exists in vivo, so coverage is, strictly,
unmeasurable on real tissue. What \emph{is} available, on a minority of protocols, is
\emph{repeatability}: a same-session ``coffee-break'' rescan that re-measures the same
tissue. Repeatability is the workhorse of the Quantitative Imaging Biomarkers Alliance
(QIBA) Profile framework, where the repeatability coefficient bounds the change in a
biomarker that exceeds measurement noise.

Repeatability is \emph{precision}, not accuracy or coverage. The central discipline of this
note is to take that distinction literally and to extract from repeatability \emph{only}
what it can license. We define a single ground-truth-free statistic on a deployed conformal
interval --- whether it is correctly \emph{sized} to scan--rescan variation --- prove that
it is invariant to bias by construction, prove that it is mathematically independent of the
existing width-\emph{ordering} check, validate it on controlled synthetic data, and then
apply it to real test--retest IVIM. The verdict is negative and sharply scoped, which is
why this work lives in \textbf{Lethe}, the project's home for honest-limitation results.

\section{Background and positioning}\label{sec:bg}
Three companion efforts frame this note; all are in review, so every claim that leans on
them is marked \PROV{}. \textbf{Gauge}\PROV{} brought split-conformal and conformalized
quantile regression to IVIM and reported, on the same ACRIN-6698 test--retest arm used
here, that the conformal interval \emph{width} rank-tracks the scan--rescan variability of
$D$ (Spearman $r=\numGaugeR$, $95\%$ CI $[\numGaugeRlo,\numGaugeRhi]$; for $\Dstar$ the
association is null, $r=\numGaugeDstar$), explicitly framing it as ``repeatability-tracking,
not validation, accuracy, calibration, or coverage.'' \textbf{Fashion}\PROV{} established
which uncertainty paradigms actually cover $\Dstar$ and supplies the calibrated posterior a
conformal width is built on. \textbf{Minos}\PROV{} prices the \emph{decision} value of a
calibrated error bar, motivating why a correctly \emph{sized} interval matters. We reuse
\textbf{Caliper} (MIT, in-tree) for the conformal ruler, read-only.

Our contribution is the axis Gauge declined: not whether width \emph{orders} repeatability,
but whether it is the right \emph{size} for it. Rank and scale are independent (\S
\ref{sec:method}); a width can order repeatability perfectly and still be the wrong
magnitude.

\section{Method}\label{sec:method}
\paragraph{Setup.} For tumor $i$ we obtain two same-session repeat estimates
$\hat\theta^{A}_i,\hat\theta^{B}_i$ of each IVIM parameter $\theta\in\{D,\Dstar,f\}$ from a
segmented plug-in fit on the sparse four-$b$ scheme $\{0,100,600,800\}\,\mathrm{s/mm^2}$,
and a deployed conformal interval $[\ell_i,u_i]$ with half-width $w_i$. All repeats are
region-level (whole-tumor ROI mean); the two acquisitions are unregistered, so no per-voxel
statement is licensed.

\paragraph{The statistic.} The headline is the symmetrized \emph{test--retest interval
coverage}
\[
c \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\Big(\mathbf{1}[\hat\theta^{B}_i\in[\ell^{A}_i,u^{A}_i]]
   + \mathbf{1}[\hat\theta^{A}_i\in[\ell^{B}_i,u^{B}_i]]\Big),
\]
reported with a BCa bootstrap $95\%$ CI over tumors. We also report the scale ratio
$R=\sigimp/\sigrep$, where $\sigimp=w/2z_{1-\alpha/2}$ is the measurement scale implied by
the interval and $\sigrep$ is the robust repeatability standard deviation estimated from the
test--retest differences.

\paragraph{Precision, not accuracy (the legitimacy razor).} Write each estimate as
$\hat\theta = \theta_{\mathrm{true}} + b + \varepsilon$, with $b$ a systematic bias and
$\varepsilon$ measurement noise. The test--retest difference
$\Delta = \hat\theta^{B}-\hat\theta^{A} = \varepsilon^{B}-\varepsilon^{A}$ \emph{cancels
$b$ exactly}. Every statistic here therefore certifies only that an interval is sized to
measurement irreproducibility (precision); it is provably blind to accuracy and makes no
ground-truth coverage claim. A consequence we use as a sanity anchor: a correctly
\emph{measurement-scaled} $90\%$ interval spans one noise draw, but test--retest coverage
asks it to span the difference of two independent draws (variance $2\sigma^2$); in the
Gaussian reference its expected test--retest coverage is
$2\Phi(z_{0.95}/\sqrt2)-1 = \numTarget$, \emph{not} $0.90$. This derivable gap between
accuracy-coverage and repeat-coverage is precisely why the two cannot be conflated.

\paragraph{Distinct from the rank check.} Gauge's statistic is the Spearman correlation of
$w$ with $|\Delta|$ --- a monotone-ordering quantity, invariant under any positive rescaling
of all widths. Our $c$ is a scale quantity: multiplying every width by a constant leaves
Spearman fixed but moves $c$ to any value in $[0,1]$. The two are independent axes of the
same object.

\section{Method self-test (SOLID)}\label{sec:selftest}
On a synthetic test--retest harness with known truth, noise, bias, and interval scale (no
upstream dependency), the statistic behaves exactly as derived (seed-fixed, $n{=}20000$):
a correctly measurement-scaled $90\%$ interval yields coverage $\numSelfScaled$ against the
analytic $\numSelfTarget$; injecting a large systematic bias leaves coverage unchanged at
$\numSelfBias$ (the precision-not-accuracy guarantee, numerically exact); and a pure width
rescale leaves the Gauge-style Spearman fixed ($\numSpearBefore\!\to\!\numSpearAfter$) while
moving coverage ($\numCovRescBefore\!\to\!\numCovRescAfter$) --- the distinctness property,
demonstrated at the unit level. The statistic is therefore a working, correctly-calibrated,
distinct validator before it ever touches real data.

$D$ (under-scaled) / $\Dstar$ (over-scaled-by-unidentifiability) split is itself the
signature that the conformal scale is not governed by repeatability.

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:disc}
\paragraph{What this validates, and what it cannot.} The method correctly \emph{measures}
interval scale against repeatability, with tight CIs and a clean sensitivity curve. It
validates precision only. It is, by construction, blind to accuracy: the real $D$ estimates
are themselves biased low by the high-$b$ Rician floor, which the statistic neither sees nor
corrects --- and should not, because that bias cancels in $\Delta$.

\paragraph{The delta versus the rank check\PROV{}.} Width \emph{rank}-tracks repeatability
($r=\numGaugeR$) \emph{and} its \emph{scale} under-covers it ($c=\numCovD$, $R=\numRD$).
These are consistent and complementary: an interval that orders repeatability correctly can
still be the wrong size for it. This is the axis Gauge declined, and the reason this is not
a duplicate of the published check.

\paragraph{Why under-scaled.} A conformal interval is calibrated, on synthetic data, to the
\emph{modeled} (thermal) noise. Real region-level test--retest variation (within-subject
$\mathrm{CV}\sim 50\%$ here) far exceeds the thermal scale because it includes repositioning,
ROI re-segmentation, and noise-floor interaction --- sources synthetic calibration cannot
see. The honest conclusion is a \emph{constrained validation}: repeatability does not
endorse the absolute scale of conventionally-calibrated conformal IVIM intervals; it can
only order, not size, them.

\paragraph{Limitations.} The exact coverage number depends on the pinned synthetic-
calibration noise model (Gaussian, thermal) and the $\sqrt{n_{\mathrm{vox}}}$ effective-SNR
assumption; a Rician-floor calibration is named future work and was \emph{not} applied post
hoc (no tuning). The direction and robustness of the result --- under-scaled across the
whole SNR grid --- do not depend on these choices. The result is region-level, not per-
voxel (unregistered repeats), and inherits all Fashion/Gauge/Minos provisional caveats.

\section{Conclusion}
Test--retest repeatability is a legitimate but strictly bounded validator: it certifies
precision, never accuracy, and it can order an interval's width without endorsing its size.
Applied to conventionally-calibrated conformal IVIM intervals, it returns a sharply-scoped
negative --- the interval for $D$ is about four times too narrow for real scan--rescan
variation --- which we record, honestly, as a Lethe result. On publication of Fashion,
Gauge, and Minos the provisional flags are discharged by a single re-run; the promotion and
fold paths are documented alongside.
