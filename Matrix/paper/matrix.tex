\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Adaptive radiotherapy is moving toward closed control loops that re-plan dose from
imaging acquired during treatment. Before such a loop can be trusted, two things must
be demonstrated separately: that the \emph{control mechanics} close, and that a
\emph{trust gate} can withhold action where the imaging-derived estimate is unreliable.
We build \textbf{Matrix}, a four-stage closed loop --- scan $\rightarrow$ posterior
$\rightarrow$ trust gate $+$ action gate $\rightarrow$ dose re-plan $\rightarrow$
re-scan --- on a \emph{synthetic digital twin} with known ground-truth intravoxel
incoherent motion (IVIM) parameters $(D,\Dstar,\fq)$ and a dose/response model. The
loop closes reproducibly: across $\numNiter$ iterations on a $\numNx{\times}\numNy$
twin, a trust gate keyed on the calibrated perfusion-fraction error bar holds the
\emph{action} rate on untrustworthy (low-SNR) voxels at $\numSupGated$\PROV{} (vs
$\numSupUngated$ [$\numSupUngatedLo$, $\numSupUngatedHi$]\PROV{} ungated) while
trustworthy voxels stay free, and trusted-tumour perfusion falls $\numDropTrust$
[$\numDropTrustLo$, $\numDropTrustHi$]\PROV{} (95\% bootstrap CI excludes 0) as the
treatment decision winds down (\numTreatTraj). The gate's separation is perfect by
construction (AUROC $\numAUROC$): the twin builds the untrustworthy zone, so this is a
property of the synthetic design, not a performance claim. We then \emph{ground} the
loop on \textbf{real} anatomy and a \textbf{real} delivered-dose grid from a public
radiotherapy dataset (TCIA \numFerryDataset, \numFerryLicense) through an
interface-only substrate swap that leaves the loop engine byte-for-byte unchanged.
The grounded loop closes (trusted-tumour drop $\numGDropTrust$
[$\numGDropTrustLo$, $\numGDropTrustHi$]\PROV{}), and it surfaces the paper's central,
honest negative: on real \emph{delivered} dose, ``holding'' an untrusted voxel no longer
protects it --- its perfusion still drops $\numFOneHeld$ [$\numFOneHeldLo$,
$\numFOneHeldHi$] (vs $\numFOneSyn$ on the synthetic baseline), because the gate
suppresses only \emph{new} actions, not dose already delivered. Action-suppression is
not outcome-protection. This is a methods demonstration: it validates the harness and a
trust-gating discipline, not any clinical effect.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
% ===========================================================================
Magnetic-resonance-guided adaptive radiotherapy (MRgRT) raises a control-systems
question that precedes any clinical one: can a re-planning loop be made to act
\emph{only where the evidence supports acting}? A loop that re-plans dose from
quantitative imaging acquired mid-treatment inherits all of that imaging's
uncertainty. Where a parameter is well identified the loop should be free to act; where
it is not, the loop should withhold action rather than chase noise. The pre-requisite
for studying that discipline is a loop whose mechanics are known to close and a trust
gate whose firing can be checked against ground truth --- neither of which requires, or
should be confused with, a real clinical result.

This paper builds and validates exactly that pre-requisite. \textbf{Matrix} is a
closed-loop harness on a \emph{synthetic digital twin}: a simulated abdominal target
with known ground-truth IVIM parameters $(D,\Dstar,\fq)$ --- where $\fq$, the perfusion
fraction, is the quantity of interest (QoI) --- and a dose/response model. The loop runs
four stages every iteration,
\[
\mbox{scan}\;\rightarrow\;\mbox{posterior}\;\rightarrow\;\mbox{trust gate}+\mbox{action gate}\;\rightarrow\;\mbox{dose re-plan}\;\rightarrow\;(\mbox{re-scan}),
\]
and the twin evolves under the delivered plan so that the next scan sees the
consequence of the last decision. Because the twin's ground truth is known and seeded,
every behaviour of the loop is falsifiable: we can ask whether the gate fires where the
twin made the estimate untrustworthy, and whether the loop converges, and answer with
confidence intervals against truth.

Matrix is deliberately scoped as a \emph{no-scanner} run-mode of a larger capstone
(``Keystone'') that runs the same loop in real time. Building Matrix first de-risks the
control logic independently of scanner, dose-engine, and regulatory access: the capstone
exists as a working artifact even if those never land. The cost of that choice is that
\emph{nothing here is a clinical claim} --- a discipline we enforce structurally
(Sec.~\ref{sec:scope}) rather than rhetorically.

We make three contributions. (i) A reproducible four-stage closed loop on a synthetic
twin, with a trust gate that withholds action where the calibrated error bar is
untrustworthy, validated end-to-end with bootstrap confidence intervals
(Sec.~\ref{sec:results-syn}). (ii) An \emph{identifiability} result: under the textbook
segmented IVIM fit, the pseudo-diffusion coefficient $\Dstar$ is not recoverable, so the
physically-motivated ``high-$\Dstar$'' sub-region cannot drive the trust gate; the gate
is instead keyed on the \emph{measured} inflation of the perfusion-fraction error bar
$\sigf$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:rekey}). (iii) A grounding of the loop on \emph{real} anatomy and
\emph{real} dose geometry through an interface-only substrate swap (\textbf{Ferry}) that
leaves the loop engine byte-for-byte unchanged, which surfaces the paper's central honest
negative: \emph{action-suppression is not outcome-protection} on a real prescription
(Sec.~\ref{sec:ferry}).

\section{Background and positioning}\label{sec:bg}
% ===========================================================================
\paragraph{IVIM perfusion as the QoI.} The intravoxel-incoherent-motion model
decomposes the diffusion-weighted signal into a tissue-diffusion term and a fast
``pseudo-diffusion'' perfusion term, parameterised by $(D,\Dstar,\fq)$. The perfusion
fraction $\fq$ is a candidate biomarker of tumour vascularity and is the QoI the loop
acts on. It is also notoriously ill-conditioned: $\Dstar$ and $\fq$ are coupled, and a
segmented two-step fit --- the standard estimator --- recovers $\Dstar$ poorly. Matrix
treats that ill-conditioning as a feature to be \emph{detected}, not hidden
(Sec.~\ref{sec:rekey}).

\paragraph{Three consumed components, each behind a clean interface.} The loop consumes
three components, none of which is final, so each sits behind a documented interface with
a clearly-labelled placeholder; the real component drops in \emph{without touching the
loop engine}.
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{Fashion} --- the calibration ruler that turns a raw posterior spread into a
\emph{calibrated} error bar with a coverage/ECE read-out (interface
\texttt{Ruler.calibrate}; placeholder \texttt{PlaceholderRuler}, NOT-Fashion). In review
at \emph{NMR in Biomedicine}; no DOI assigned.
\item \textbf{Minos} --- the trust gate (value of a trusted gate, VoTG) and the
treat/spare/escalate action gate (interfaces \texttt{TrustGate}, \texttt{ActionGate};
placeholders NOT-Minos). Theory half machine-verified; applied half in review.
\item \textbf{Forge} --- the Monte-Carlo dose engine (interface \texttt{DoseEngine};
placeholder \texttt{PlaceholderDoseEngine}, NOT-Forge). \textbf{Not built} --- Forge today
is only a dose-simulation feasibility benchmark; the re-plan engine is deferred to 2027.
Forge is therefore presented as drop-in future work, not a dependency met.
\end{itemize}
Every number that rides on Fashion or Minos is marked \PROV. Forge's placeholder affects
only the \emph{physical realism} of the re-plan, not whether the loop closes or how the
gates behave; it is not a release condition (Sec.~\ref{sec:scope}).

\paragraph{The synthetic/real boundary.} Matrix's default substrate is fully synthetic.
The optional \textbf{Ferry} substrate (Sec.~\ref{sec:ferry}) makes \emph{anatomy} and
\emph{dose geometry} real (a public RT dataset), while \emph{perfusion/IVIM stays
synthetic} --- there is no scanner, hence no real diffusion data. We state which loop
quantities are real and which are synthetic at every results claim, and never blur the two.

\section{Methods}\label{sec:method}
% ===========================================================================
\subsection{The synthetic digital twin}
The twin is a $\numNx{\times}\numNy$ abdominal slice ($\numNvox$ voxels) with a central
tumour disk, an organ-at-risk corner, and normal tissue elsewhere. Per-voxel ground-truth
$(D,\Dstar,\fq)$ are drawn from tissue priors with seeded jitter; a reproducible subset of
tumour voxels is pushed into a high-$\Dstar$ regime (a vigorous-perfusion core). All
randomness derives from a single master seed (\numSeed), so two twins built from the same
seed are bit-for-bit identical in every field --- the reproducibility contract checked at
gate CP1. The twin exposes itself to the loop through exactly three calls: \texttt{scan},
\texttt{truth\_snapshot} (read-only, for evaluation), and \texttt{apply\_plan} (which
delivers a new dose and evolves the ground truth).

\paragraph{Synthetic acquisition and degradation.} A scan is a biexponential IVIM
forward model over nine $b$-values with Rician noise at nominal SNR $\numSNR$, repeated
to give a raw posterior spread. A vertical band clips the tumour where local SNR collapses
to $\numSNRlow$ --- the \emph{acquisition-degradation zone}. There the measured error bar
on $\fq$ genuinely inflates; this is the honest, \emph{measured} signal the trust gate
fires on (Sec.~\ref{sec:rekey}).

\paragraph{Dose/response.} A voxel boosted above baseline loses perfusion,
$\fq \leftarrow \fq \, e^{-\beta\, b_{\mathrm{frac}}}$, where $b_{\mathrm{frac}}$ is the
boost fraction; sparing or holding leaves $\fq$ unchanged. This is the mechanism that
lets the loop \emph{converge}: treated tumour perfusion decays toward the spare threshold,
after which the action gate stops boosting. It is a synthetic response model, not a
radiobiological claim.

\subsection{Posterior stage (Matrix's own estimator)}
Each scan is fit with the textbook two-step \emph{segmented} IVIM method: a high-$b$
log-linear fit for $D$ and the intercept-implied $\fq$, then a low-$b$ residual fit for
$\Dstar$. Fitting every noise realisation gives a raw per-voxel posterior centre $\mu$ and
spread $\sigma$ for $(D,\Dstar,\fq)$. The estimator is deliberately the standard segmented
method so that $\Dstar$ (and, through the $\fq$--$\Dstar$ coupling, $\fq$) is genuinely
ill-conditioned in the high-$\Dstar$ regime: nothing is hand-tuned to fake that
(Sec.~\ref{sec:rekey}).

\subsection{The four-stage loop}\label{sec:loop}
A shared per-iteration \texttt{LoopState} is threaded through four pure stages:
(1) \emph{scan} reads the truth snapshot and acquires a noisy scan; (2) \emph{posterior}
fits the raw $(\mu,\sigma)$ and applies the \textbf{ruler} to produce a calibrated
$\sigf$ with coverage/ECE; (3) \emph{gates} apply the \textbf{trust gate} (which voxels
are trustworthy) and the \textbf{action gate} (treat/spare/escalate, with action
suppressed where untrustworthy); (4) \emph{dose} applies the \textbf{dose engine}'s
re-plan and delivers it, evolving the twin. The three consumed components are bundled in
a single \texttt{Interfaces} object; swapping a real component for its placeholder is a
one-line change \emph{in the bundle}, never in a stage. This is the structural property
Ferry later exploits (Sec.~\ref{sec:ferry}).

\paragraph{Trust gate.} A voxel is flagged untrustworthy when its calibrated
perfusion-fraction error bar is too wide ($\sigf > \sigma_{f,\max}$) or the fit lands in
the high-$\Dstar$ identifiability regime. The action gate is a Bayes-action-shaped
interval rule on $\fq$ --- TREAT if the calibrated interval lies confidently above the
treat threshold, SPARE if confidently below a spare threshold, ESCALATE otherwise --- and
the trust gate then \emph{suppresses action} by forcing every untrustworthy voxel to
ESCALATE regardless of the raw decision. The pre-suppression decision is recorded so the
gate's effect can be measured.

\subsection{Evaluation}
Every load-bearing summary number is reported with a seeded bootstrap confidence interval;
the trust gate's separation is summarised by the rank statistic AUROC of the calibrated
$\sigf$ against the (known) low-SNR label. All metrics operate only on the twin's known
ground truth and the per-iteration state records; they make no clinical claim.

\subsection{Interface design and the byte-identity anchor}\label{sec:byte}
The loop engine \texttt{run\_iteration} references the twin only as a parameter and the
three components only through their interfaces. This is what makes both the
component-swap (placeholder $\rightarrow$ real Fashion/Minos/Forge) and the
substrate-swap (synthetic twin $\rightarrow$ Ferry's grounded twin) possible without
editing a single loop stage. We make the substrate-swap claim concrete and falsifiable:
the loop engine's source file is \emph{byte-for-byte identical} across the synthetic and
grounded runs (git blob \texttt{\numLoopBlob}), asserted by the Ferry drop-in gate
(Sec.~\ref{sec:ferry}).

\section{Results: the synthetic twin}\label{sec:results-syn}
% ===========================================================================
All synthetic-twin results use a $\numNx{\times}\numNy$ twin, seed \numSeed, $\numNiter$
iterations, nominal SNR $\numSNR$ (low-SNR zone $\numSNRlow$), with 95\% bootstrap CIs.

\subsection{The loop closes}
The four stages run every iteration, the twin evolves under the delivered plan, and a
re-run from the same seed is bit-identical: the loop closes reproducibly and end-to-end.
This is a property of the harness and twin and does not depend on any consumed component.

\subsection{The calibrated error bar}
The ruler\PROV{} brings the perfusion-fraction error bar to near-nominal coverage on the
twin: 95\% empirical coverage $\numCovF$ [$\numCovFlo$, $\numCovFhi$] (seeded bootstrap CI
over voxels) with expected calibration error $\mathrm{ECE}_f=\numECEf$. This number rides
on the Fashion placeholder and is marked \PROV; the real Fashion ruler drops in behind the
same interface.

\subsection{The trust gate, and why AUROC $=\numAUROC$ is a property of the design}
\label{sec:auroc}
The trust gate flags the untrustworthy low-SNR zone with AUROC $\numAUROC$ of the
calibrated $\sigf$ against the known low-SNR label, firing at rate $\numFireLow$ inside
the zone and $\numFireGood$ (CI hi $\numFireGoodHi$) outside.

\noindent\emph{Scoping.} An AUROC of $\numAUROC$ is \textbf{not} a performance claim. The
synthetic twin \emph{constructs} the untrustworthy zone (the low-SNR band) and the gate is
keyed on the error bar that the same zone inflates; the two are separable \emph{by
construction}. The right reading is: ``when an untrustworthy region is present and
measurable, the gate keyed on the measured error bar identifies it,'' which is a sanity
check on the harness, not evidence that any gate would separate trustworthy from
untrustworthy voxels in real data. The value of grounding on real geometry
(Sec.~\ref{sec:ferry}) is precisely that it stops this number from being read as more than
it is.

\subsection{Action suppression}
Across the whole run, the trust gate holds the \emph{action} rate on untrustworthy voxels
at $\numSupGated$\PROV{}, versus $\numSupUngated$ [$\numSupUngatedLo$, $\numSupUngatedHi$]\PROV{}
without the gate, while trustworthy voxels stay free to act ($\numSupTrust$
[$\numSupTrustLo$, $\numSupTrustHi$]\PROV{}). The gate has a real, measured effect and does
not over-suppress the trustworthy population.

\subsection{Convergence}
Trusted-tumour perfusion falls $\numDropTrust$ [$\numDropTrustLo$, $\numDropTrustHi$]\PROV{}
over the loop (CI excludes 0) while the held untrusted tumour does not move
($\numDropHeld$): the honest cost of untrustworthiness is that those voxels are not
treated. The treatment decision winds down --- TREAT actions \numTreatTraj{} --- as mean
true perfusion falls from $\numFtruthFirst$ to $\numFtruthLast$ and dose de-escalation is
bounded by the floor ($\numDoseMin$~Gy). Figure~\ref{fig:syn} shows the trust gate firing on
the measured low-SNR zone and the closed-loop trajectory; Table~\ref{tab:syn} collects the
load-bearing numbers.

\subsection{An identifiability result: $\Dstar$ cannot drive the gate; the gate is
re-keyed on $\sigf$}\label{sec:rekey}
The twin embeds a physically-motivated high-$\Dstar$ sub-region, and one might expect the
trust gate to flag it as untrustworthy. It cannot --- and this is the point. Under the
segmented fit, $\Dstar$ is not identifiable: the low-$b$ residual that carries $\Dstar$ is
dominated by noise, so the estimated $\Dstar$ does not track the true high-$\Dstar$ label.
A gate keyed on estimated $\Dstar$ would therefore be keyed on noise. The working trust
signal is instead the \emph{measured} inflation of the calibrated perfusion-fraction error
bar $\sigf$ in the low-SNR zone --- which is why the AUROC in Sec.~\ref{sec:auroc} is
computed on $\sigf$, not on $\Dstar$. We state this as an \emph{identifiability} result
scoped to the segmented-fit regime: high-$\Dstar$ is the \emph{failed} trust driver, and
$\sigf$ is the one that works. A different estimator (e.g.\ a full Bayesian fit) could
change what is identifiable; the claim is about the segmented fit, not about IVIM in
general.

\section{Results: grounding on real anatomy and dose (Ferry)}\label{sec:ferry}
% ===========================================================================
To pre-empt the objection that the loop is only shown on a tidy synthetic twin, we ground
it on \textbf{real} anatomy and a \textbf{real} delivered-dose grid from a public
radiotherapy dataset --- TCIA \numFerryDataset{} (DOI \texttt{\numFerryDOI},
\numFerryLicense), patient \texttt{\numFerryPatient}, an axial slice at $z=\numFerrySliceZ$~mm
with real delivered dose in [\numFerryDoseLo, \numFerryDoseHi]~Gy. The grounded substrate
(``Ferry'') replaces the twin's synthetic anatomy and dose with the dataset's RTSTRUCT
contours and RTDOSE grid; \emph{perfusion/IVIM remains synthetic}, drawn with the
identical seeded mechanism as the twin, so the \emph{only} differences from the synthetic
run are the two real fields.

\paragraph{Drop-in, proven byte-for-byte.} Ferry is an interface-only swap: its grounded
twin satisfies the same three-call contract the loop consumes, so it is handed to the
\emph{same} loop engine. We assert this structurally --- the loop engine source is
byte-for-byte identical to the synthetic run (git blob \texttt{\numLoopBlob}) --- so any
behaviour change is attributable to real geometry and nothing else.

\paragraph{The loop closes on real geometry.} On a $\numFerryGrid{\times}\numFerryGrid$
matched grid, the grounded loop closes: it runs end-to-end and reproducibly; the trust
gate holds the untrustworthy \emph{action} rate at $\numGSupGated$\PROV{} (vs
$\numGSupUngated$ [$\numGSupUngatedLo$, $\numGSupUngatedHi$]\PROV{} ungated); trusted-tumour
perfusion falls $\numGDropTrust$ [$\numGDropTrustLo$, $\numGDropTrustHi$]\PROV{} (CI excludes
0); and the treatment decision winds down (\numGTreatTraj). Real anatomy is larger and
irregular ($\numGTumorVox$ vs $\numSTumorVox$ tumour voxels;
$\numGTrust$/$\numGUntrust$ trusted/untrusted), and the loop's qualitative behaviour is
preserved. The harness is not an artefact of the synthetic twin's tidy geometry.

\subsection{The central honest negative: action-suppression is not
outcome-protection}\label{sec:f1}
The grounded run surfaces a finding the synthetic twin cannot show, and it is the most
important result in this paper (Fig.~\ref{fig:ferry}).

voxels, which the twin's (synthetic) dose/response model then turns into a perfusion
decline. The drop is synthetic perfusion responding to \emph{real dose geometry}, not a
measured biological effect; what matters is the loop logic it exposes. The trust gate
suppresses \emph{new actions}; it cannot undo dose already delivered.

\noindent The consequence is sharp: \textbf{action-suppression is not the same as
outcome-protection}. A trust gate that withholds re-planning still leaves an untrusted
voxel exposed to whatever dose the standing plan delivers. This is visible only once the
dose geometry is real --- on a flat synthetic baseline the held drop is identically zero,
which is exactly why a synthetic-only study would have missed it. For an adaptive loop the
implication is that trust-gating the \emph{decision} is necessary but not sufficient;
protecting an outcome additionally requires control over the standing prescription, which
is the dose engine's responsibility (Forge, future work). Table~\ref{tab:ferry} places the
finding in its side-by-side context. Crucially, this negative does \emph{not} depend on the
Fashion or Minos placeholders being correct: it follows from real delivered dose plus the
gate's structural property of acting only on \emph{new} actions, so it survives a faithful
drop-in of the real components.

\paragraph{What a reviewer can and cannot conclude.} A reviewer \emph{can} conclude that
Matrix's closed loop closes on real geometry. A reviewer \emph{cannot} conclude anything
about real perfusion or IVIM: the $(D,\Dstar,\fq)$ ground truth, the scan, and the SNR map
are synthetic. Ferry closes the \emph{geometry} gap; the residual \emph{diffusion} gap ---
real multi-$b$ acquisition on the same anatomy --- is closed only by a scanner, which is
Keystone's real-time/offline mode and explicitly out of scope here.

% ===========================================================================
\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}
% ===========================================================================
\paragraph{The honest negative is the contribution.} It would be easy to report only the
positives: the loop closes, the gate separates perfectly, perfusion falls under treatment.
The most useful result is the negative one. F1 (Sec.~\ref{sec:f1}) says that a trust gate,
however well it suppresses action, does not by itself protect an untrusted voxel from a
standing dose --- and it says so with a CI that excludes zero, on real delivered dose,
against a synthetic baseline that is identically zero. Surfacing that distinction early,
on a synthetic-plus-grounded harness, is cheaper and safer than discovering it in a
clinical loop.

\paragraph{Identifiability, not impossibility.} The $\Dstar$ result
(Sec.~\ref{sec:rekey}) is scoped: under the segmented fit, $\Dstar$ is not a valid trust
driver, so the gate is re-keyed on the measured $\sigf$. This is an identifiability
statement about a specific estimator, not a claim that $\Dstar$ is unknowable in
principle. A richer posterior could change what is identifiable; the harness is built so
that swapping the estimator is a localized change.

\paragraph{What is solid and what is provisional.} ``The loop closes'' --- the harness,
the twin, the segmented posterior, and the Ferry geometry result --- is solid for what it
claims. The calibration, trust, and convergence \emph{numbers} consume the Fashion ruler
and Minos gates and are provisional (\PROV) until those publish; their interfaces are
fixed, so a faithful drop-in re-runs the same gates. The F1 negative is robust to that
drop-in by construction. \textbf{Forge} (the dose engine) is not built; its placeholder
affects only the physical realism of the re-plan (e.g.\ a NOT-Forge warrant artefact on a
non-uniform real prescription), never whether the loop closes or how the gates behave. We
present Forge as drop-in future work, not a dependency met, and it is not a release
condition for this manuscript.

\paragraph{Relation to Keystone.} Matrix is the no-scanner run-mode of a closed-loop
capstone. Its value is that the control logic and the trust-gating discipline can be
validated --- and their honest limits surfaced --- before any scanner, dose engine, or
clinical data is in hand.

% ===========================================================================
\section{Scope ceiling and limitations}\label{sec:scope}
% ===========================================================================
We restate the boundary because it is load-bearing. (1) \emph{No clinical claim.} Every
result means ``the loop closes and behaves sensibly,'' on a synthetic twin or on real
geometry with synthetic perfusion. (2) \emph{Synthetic perfusion everywhere.} Even under
Ferry, $(D,\Dstar,\fq)$, the scan, and the SNR map are synthetic; there is no scanner and
no real diffusion data. (3) \emph{AUROC $=\numAUROC$ is by construction}
(Sec.~\ref{sec:auroc}), not a performance claim. (4) \emph{Provisional components.}
Calibration/trust/convergence numbers ride on Fashion and Minos (in review) and are marked
\PROV; the dose engine (Forge) is not built. (5) \emph{Identifiability is scoped} to the
segmented fit (Sec.~\ref{sec:rekey}). The single residual gap that turns any perfusion
reading into a clinical statement --- real IVIM acquisition --- is left to a future scanner
study.

% ===========================================================================
\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
% ===========================================================================
Matrix closes a four-stage adaptive-radiotherapy control loop on a synthetic digital twin,
withholds action where the calibrated perfusion-fraction error bar is untrustworthy, and
converges --- all reproducibly and with confidence intervals against known ground truth. It
grounds the same loop, byte-for-byte unchanged, on real anatomy and real dose geometry, and
in doing so surfaces an honest negative that a synthetic-only study would miss:
action-suppression is not outcome-protection on a real prescription. As a methods
demonstration it validates a harness and a trust-gating discipline --- and their limits ---
not any clinical effect.
